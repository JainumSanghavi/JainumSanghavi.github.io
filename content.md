## PROJECT DESCRIPTIONS

### 1. Equivariant Traction Force Prediction
Designed SO(2)-equivariant neural architectures that predict cellular traction forces from 2D displacement fields, achieving a 7-degree mean angular error — without any data augmentation. Built on group theory and representation theory foundations, this system leverages continuous rotation and translation equivariance to enforce physical symmetries directly in the network, producing predictions that are both accurate and geometrically consistent.

### 2. State Spaces vs Transformers — Language Modeling Benchmark
Conducted a rigorous head-to-head benchmark of decoder-only Transformers against Structured State Spaces (S4) on the BookCorpus dataset, measuring perplexity scores of 4.02 and 5.1, respectively. The study revealed that while Transformers maintain superiority for large-vocabulary language modeling, S4 models remain highly competitive through FFT-based sequence modeling — offering meaningful trade-offs in computational efficiency for long-range sequence tasks.

### 3. Stochastic Warehouse Management Optimization
Engineered a stochastic inventory allocation system that achieved a 90% cost reduction over a random baseline by combining Q-learning, simulated annealing, and genetic algorithms for spatial optimization. The system intelligently allocates products across warehouse zones to minimize picking costs, with interactive 3D visualizations built in PyVista for real-time decision support and strategy evaluation.

### 4. Latent Compression With Autoencoders
Built deep autoencoder architectures that learn compact latent representations of facial images, achieving 30 dB PSNR reconstruction quality on the CelebA dataset. The system demonstrates how learned compression can preserve perceptual fidelity while dramatically reducing dimensionality, with practical applications in efficient image storage and transmission.
